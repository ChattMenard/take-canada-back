# Evidence Web

## Interconnection Matrix for Veritas Accountability Framework

This document maps the relationships between policies, corporations, government bodies, and economic mechanisms across all Veritas analyses. The goal is to surface hidden connections that appear separate on the surface but share common beneficiaries, common victims, and common root causes.

---

## Core Thesis

Canada's economic and policy architecture is designed to **extract value from public resources and existing affordable assets, convert them into corporate profit, and externalize the costs onto Canadian workers, tenants, and communities.**

Every analysis in this framework points to the same mechanism operating across different sectors:

- **Housing:** Destroy below-market stock, replace with higher-revenue density
- **Energy:** Export raw bitumen, import refined products at markup
- **Tax:** Allow profitable corporations to pay minimal rates, shift burden to workers
- **Labour:** Roll back worker protections when they interfere with corporate operations
- **Rail:** Crown-chartered infrastructure repurposed for private profit on resource exports

## Cross-Sector Connections

### Connection 1: Revenue Recirculation

```
Corporate tax increase ($43.5B potential)
    ↓
Funds public infrastructure purchase / construction
    ↓
    ├─→ Crown-owned refineries ($8.5B) → fuel sovereignty
    ├─→ Public housing (500K units, $150B over 10 yrs)
    ├─→ High-speed rail ($12-15B) → reduces CN/CPR dependency
    ├─→ Crown bank expansion → reduces Big Five mortgage dependency
    └─→ Pharmacare / dental care ($5-7B/yr)
```

### Connection 2: The Export Loop

```
Canadian resources (bitumen, grain, potash, timber)
    ↓
CN / CPR haul to port or pipeline (tolls/profit to railway)
    ↓
Exported raw to US or global market (profit to foreign processors)
    ↓
Processed / refined product imported back (price = global + transport + margin)
    ↓
Canadian consumer pays final price
    ↓
Profit margins flow to: US refiners, foreign commodity traders, railway shareholders
```

### Connection 3: Housing as Resource Extraction

```
Post-war affordable housing (below-market rent, mature neighbourhood)
    ↓
Upzoning policy allows 8 units where 1 stood
    ↓
Developer buys lot, demolishes house, builds row house
    ↓
GIM assessment inflates value based on 8-unit rent potential
    ↓
City gets 4x tax revenue; developer books construction profit; landlord gets 6-7x rental revenue
    ↓
Original tenant displaced; below-market stock permanently destroyed; community character erased
    ↓
Profit flows to: developer, landlord, city treasury
```

### Connection 4: Policy Capture Feedback Loop

```
Corporate profit → Political donations + lobbying
    ↓
Policy decisions favouring corporate interests
    ├─→ Corporate tax kept low (27% blended)
    ├─→ Multi-res tax premium phased out
    ├─→ No below-market housing mandate
    ├─→ Pipeline approvals enabling raw export
    ├─→ OHS thresholds raised to prevent work stoppages
    └─→ Bank of Canada board seats for bankers
    ↓
More corporate profit → More political influence → Loop repeats
```

### Connection 5: The Crown Corporation Paradox

| Crown Corp / Charter | Original Purpose | Current Reality |
|---------------------|------------------|-----------------|
| **CN (1919 nationalization)** | Public rail for Canadian sovereignty | For-profit, 40% US revenue, hauls resources south |
| **CPR (Dominion charter)** | Transcontinental nation-building | Merged with KCS, 50% US revenue, private profit |
| **Bank of Canada (1938)** | Monetary policy for public interest | Board stacked with Big Five bankers |
| **CMHC** | Affordable housing + mortgage insurance | Enables developer finance; no below-market mandate |
| **Hydro-Québec / SaskTel / ATB** | Public utility / banking | Actual public ownership models that work; outliers |

The pattern: entities created to serve Canadian public interest have been repurposed to serve private profit, with the exception of Quebec and Saskatchewan outliers that prove public ownership is viable.

## Strategic Insights from the Web

### 1. The Common Mechanism

Every sector operates on the same extraction principle:
- Take something that exists (bitumen, affordable housing, worker protections, tax revenue)
- Convert it to private profit (refining margin, rental revenue, uninterrupted production, retained earnings)
- Externalize the cost (imported fuel, displaced tenants, injured workers, public deficit)

### 2. The Leverage Point

The **proposed progressive corporate tax** is the single policy that touches every Extractor and every Enabler:
- Hits CN, CPR, Irving, Big Five, foreign oil majors, developers, REITs
- Generates $10.5-43.5B annually
- Funds the public ownership alternative that breaks every loop

### 3. The Reframing

Instead of fighting each battle separately (housing, energy, tax, labour), the web shows they are **the same battle.**

- Housing infill and fuel exports are the same mechanism: extract existing value, privatize the gain, socialize the loss.
- Corporate tax avoidance and multi-res tax phase-out are the same mechanism: shift burden from profitable entities to working households.
- OHS rollback and pipeline export policy are the same mechanism: worker and public welfare subordinated to corporate operations.

The response should be unified:
- **Tax the extractors at the top bracket**
- **Use the revenue to buy what they leave behind**
- **Run it publicly, for public benefit**
