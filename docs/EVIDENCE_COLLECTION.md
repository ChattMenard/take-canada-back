## Category 1: Active Legislative Threats (HIGHEST PRIORITY)

### Bill C-22 — Lawful Access Act

**Threat:** Ministerial power to compel service providers to build surveillance
backdoors without parliamentary oversight. Standing warrant for infrastructure
backdoors by ministerial signature.

**Legal relevance:** Charter s.7 (life, liberty, security), s.8 (unreasonable
search/seizure), s.2(b) (freedom of expression via encryption chilling effect).
Administrative law: ministerial overreach, parliamentary bypass.

## Category 2: Emergency Powers (2022 Invocation)

### Emergencies Act 2022

**Threat:** First peacetime use of the Emergencies Act against domestic protest.
Financial coercion without court order — bank accounts frozen by donation
history, not criminal charge.

**Legal relevance:** Charter s.2(c) (freedom of assembly), s.2(d) (freedom of
association), s.7 (life, liberty, security), s.8 (unreasonable search/seizure
of financial records). Administrative law: ultra vires (did the legal threshold
for invocation exist?), procedural fairness (no hearing before account freeze).

## Category 3: COVID Emergency Powers Infrastructure

### Food and Drugs Act s.30.1 — Interim Order Power

**Threat:** Minister of Health can bypass normal drug authorization with one
signature. Built post-SARS 2003, used for COVID vaccines, mRNA classified as
biologic drugs not gene therapies.

**Legal relevance:** Charter s.7 (life, liberty, security of person — bodily
autonomy, medical consent). Administrative law: ultra vires (did interim orders
exceed statutory authority?), procedural fairness (no consent, no hearing).
