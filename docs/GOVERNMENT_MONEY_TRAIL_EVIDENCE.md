## Proof Framework: What Constitutes Evidence

### **Level 1 Evidence: Documented Facts**
- Official asset declarations
- Government contract records
- Policy announcement dates
- Corporate registry filings

### **Level 2 Evidence: Temporal Correlations**
- Asset purchases before policy announcements (30-180 days)
- Business establishments before regulatory changes
- Contract awards to newly-formed companies
- Policy decisions benefiting held assets

### **Level 3 Evidence: Network Connections**
- Family business relationships
- Political donation patterns
- Revolving door employment
- Business associate networks

### **Level 4 Evidence: Statistical Patterns**
- Significant timing advantages (p < 0.05)
- Consistent patterns across multiple officials
- Market vs. policy-driven asset movements
- Correlation strength and significance

## Specific Proof Examples We're Seeking

### **Pattern A: Pre-Policy Asset Acquisition**
**What to Find:**
- Official purchases stock 60 days before beneficial policy announcement
- Official involved in policy decision-making process
- Stock in sector directly affected by policy
- Significant value increase post-announcement

**Evidence Required:**
- Asset declaration showing purchase date
- Cabinet minutes showing official involvement
- Policy announcement date
- Stock price data showing value increase

### **Pattern B: Family Business Policy Benefits**
**What to Find:**
- Spouse starts business 90 days before regulatory change
- Business directly benefits from new regulation
- Official involved in regulatory development
- Rapid business growth post-implementation

**Evidence Required:**
- Business registration date
- Regulatory change timeline
- Official's role in process
- Business revenue growth data

### **Pattern C: Procurement Contract Connections**
**What to Find:**
- Contract awarded to former colleague's company
- Sole-source contract without competitive bidding
- Official involved in procurement decision
- Company established shortly before contract

**Evidence Required:**
- Contract award documents
- Company ownership structure
- Procurement decision records
- Company establishment date

## Verification and Quality Control

### **Evidence Verification Protocol**
1. **Source Verification:** Confirm document authenticity
2. **Date Verification:** Cross-reference timeline accuracy
3. **Content Verification:** Ensure factual accuracy
4. **Chain of Custody:** Maintain evidence integrity

### **Quality Control Standards**
- Multiple source verification for key facts
- Statistical significance testing for patterns
- Peer review of analysis methods
- Documentation of all assumptions

### **Legal Compliance**
- Focus on public records only
- Use qualified language ("appears to suggest" vs. "proves")
- Avoid speculation about motives
- Stick to documented evidence
