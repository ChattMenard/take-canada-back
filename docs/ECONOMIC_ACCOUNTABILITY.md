# Economic Accountability Framework

## Bridging Economic Analysis to Government Accountability

This document connects our quantitative economic research with the Veritas transparency campaign, providing a framework for evidence collection and accountability requests focused on economic policy and fiscal responsibility.

---

## Purpose

Economic accountability extends government oversight beyond traditional transparency tracks to include:

1. **Fiscal Responsibility** - Understanding how tax policy affects deficits and public services
2. **Corporate Tax Transparency** - Who pays what and why in the Canadian tax system
3. **Energy Independence** - Policy barriers to fuel sovereignty and economic security
4. **Wealth Distribution** - How economic policies affect different segments of society

---

## Economic Evidence Categories

### 1. Deficit and Fiscal Policy Evidence

**Key Questions:**
- How did the deficit double from $36.3B to $55.3B in one year?
- What policy decisions drove the $68.4B net new spending for 2025-2031?
- How do corporate tax changes affect deficit reduction?

**Evidence Types:**
- Treasury Board fiscal analyses
- Department of Finance deficit projections
- Parliamentary Budget Office reports
- Cabinet meeting minutes on fiscal decisions
- Inter-departmental communications on spending priorities

### 2. Corporate Tax Structure Evidence

**Key Questions:**
- How many corporations profit >$20M annually from Canadian operations?
- What is the current revenue distribution by profit bracket?
- How would progressive tax rates impact government revenue?

**Evidence Types:**
- CRA corporate tax return statistics
- Revenue modeling scenarios
- Tax policy development documents
- Corporate tax compliance data
- International tax comparison studies

- Policy barriers affect economic competitiveness

**Economic Justice:**
- Tax policies affect wealth distribution
- Government spending priorities reflect societal values
- Economic decisions should be evidence-based

### Media Framing Guidelines

**DO SAY:**
- "Evidence-based fiscal policy analysis"
- "Corporate tax fairness and revenue generation"
- "Energy security and economic independence"
- "Distributional impact of economic policies"
- "Transparent decision-making processes"

**DON'T SAY:**
- "Tax conspiracy theories"
- "Government corruption allegations"
- "Secret economic plots"
- "Proof of malicious intent"

## Conclusion

Economic accountability extends government transparency to include fiscal responsibility, tax fairness, and energy security. By integrating quantitative economic analysis with the Veritas evidence framework, we provide citizens with the tools to hold governments accountable for economic decisions that affect every Canadian.

This framework ensures that economic policy is subject to the same rigorous evidence collection and verification standards as other areas of government accountability, creating a comprehensive approach to democratic oversight.
