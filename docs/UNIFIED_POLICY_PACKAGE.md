# Unified Policy Package: From Extraction to Sovereignty

## A Single Framework Connecting Tax, Ownership, Housing, Labour, and Banking

This document unifies the TAKE_CANADA_BACK accountability analyses into one actionable policy architecture. The goal is to show how **progressive corporate taxation** generates the revenue to **purchase public ownership** of strategic infrastructure, which in turn **restores housing affordability, energy sovereignty, worker protections, and financial independence** — while remaining fiscally neutral or positive.

---

## The Core Mechanism

```text
Progressive corporate tax (30-37.5% on >$20M profit)
    ↓
Generates $10.5B – $43.5B annually (static estimate; dynamic estimate accounting for behavioral response would be lower)
    ↓
    ├─→ Crown refineries + fuel sovereignty
    ├─→ Public housing construction (500K units / 10 yr)
    ├─→ Crown banking expansion (ATB model, national)
    ├─→ High-speed rail (Toronto-Montreal corridor)
    ├─→ Worker protection restoration (reverse Bill 47, expand OHS)
    └─→ Universal pharmacare + dental care
    ↓
Public ownership reduces extraction → Less corporate profit to tax
    ↓
Lower cost of living → Higher disposable income → Stronger local economy
    ↓
Reduced corporate dependency → Reduced political capture → Policy democracy
```

**The economy does not shrink when corporations leave — it democratizes.**

## Pillar 2: Crown-Owned Refining — Fuel Sovereignty

### The Problem

- Canada exports **4.0 million barrels/day** of raw bitumen to the US
- Canada imports **refined products** back at global price + transport + margin
- US Gulf Coast refiners capture **$14.6B – $21.9B/year** in crack spread on Canadian crude
- Atlantic Canada pays the highest fuel prices despite being an oil-producing nation

### The Solution

| Project | Cost | Timeline | Revenue Source |
|---------|------|----------|---------------|
| 1-2 heavy-crude upgraders + refineries (brownfield expansion / acquisition of distressed assets) | **$8.5B** | 8-12 years | ~1 year of multinational tax revenue |
  - *Note: New greenfield refinery construction in North America typically costs $10–15B+ per facility. The $8.5B figure here assumes brownfield expansion, retrofit, or acquisition of existing distressed refining capacity, not new greenfield construction.*
| Refurbish / expand Sarnia + Montreal corridor | ~$3-5B | 4-6 years | Partial year |
| Atlantic Crown refinery (process Canadian crude for Atlantic Canadians) | ~$2-3B | 6-8 years | Partial year |

### Impact

- **~13% reduction** in pump prices (per `TRANSPARENCY_CAMPAIGN.md`)
- Refining jobs in **Alberta, Ontario, New Brunswick** — not Texas
- Margin retained in Canada as **Crown profit**, not shareholder dividends
- Sovereign control of domestic supply during geopolitical disruption

Source: `FUEL_CIRCLE_JERK.md`, `TRANSPARENCY_CAMPAIGN.md`

## Pillar 3: Public Housing Construction

### The Problem: Housing Affordability

- Infill policy **destroys below-market stock** (e.g., $1,700/mo 2BR with yard → demolished for 8 units at $1,350+/mo without bills)
- No national inventory of demolitions by affordability tier
- No mandatory below-market replacement
- GIM assessment values properties on **rent extraction potential**, not replacement cost

### The Solution: Public Housing at Scale

| Project | Cost | Timeline | Revenue Source |
|---------|------|----------|---------------|
| 500,000 public / non-profit housing units | **$150B over 10 years** ($15B/year) | 10 years | $15B/year from full $43.5B tax revenue |
| Community land trust expansion | ~$2B/year | Ongoing | Partial allocation |
| Below-market replacement mandate | Regulatory (zero capital) | Immediate | Policy change |

### Impact

- **Replaces destroyed below-market stock** at scale
- Rents set at **cost-plus**, not market rate
- Permanent removal from speculative market
- Models: Vienna social housing (60% of residents), Singapore HDB (80% homeownership)

Source: `CORPORATE_HOUSING_INFLUENCE.md`

## Pillar 4: Crown Banking Expansion

### The Problem: Financial Capture

- The **Big Five banks** (RBC, TD, BMO, Scotiabank, CIBC) sit on the Bank of Canada board and set monetary policy that favors mortgage profitability
- No public alternative for consumer banking at national scale
- Payday lending, high-interest fintech, and predatory mortgage products fill the gap

### The Solution: Crown Banking

| Model | Status | Expansion |
|-------|--------|-----------|
| **Alberta Treasury Branches (ATB)** | Provincial Crown bank since 1938, $17B assets | **Nationalize / expand to all provinces** |
| **SaskTel** | Crown telecom, comparable service, lower cost | Model for Crown banking tech infrastructure |
| **Hydro-Québec** | Public utility, world-class, low cost | Model for public service delivery |

### Impact

- Mortgages at **cost-plus** rather than market rate
- No shareholder profit extraction from Canadian debt
- **Postal banking** option in every community with a post office
- Reduced household debt burden → more disposable income → stronger local economy

Source: `EVIDENCE_WEB.md`

## Pillar 5: Worker Protection Restoration

### The Problem: Eroded Worker Protections

- **Bill 47 (Alberta, 2020):** Raised OHS work-refusal threshold to "undue hazard" — preventing workers from refusing unsafe work unless threat is immediate and serious
- **Critical worker exemption (Jan 2022):** Allowed asymptomatic COVID+ workers to be recalled by employer discretion
- **Private sector union density:** 16% (down from ~35% in 1980s)
- Worker safety subordinated to **quarterly earnings pressure**

### The Solution: Restore Worker Power

| Policy | Mechanism | Cost |
|--------|-----------|------|
| Reverse Bill 47 OHS threshold | Restore pre-2020 "dangerous work" standard | Regulatory |
| Expand sectoral bargaining | Allow unions to bargain across an entire sector, not just one employer | Regulatory |
| Crown corporation union preference | New public enterprises bargain collectively by design | Built into ownership model |
| National paid sick leave | 10 days minimum, employer-funded | ~$2B/year (offset by reduced turnover, healthier workforce) |

### Impact

- Public sector unionization rate is **71%** vs. private sector **16%**
- Public ownership **expands union coverage** by design
- Parliamentary accountability means safety practices are subject to **ATIP, committee review, and ministerial oversight** — not quarterly earnings pressure

Source: `CORPORATE_FLIGHT_REBUTTAL.md`

## Pillar 6: High-Speed Rail + Transit Sovereignty

### The Problem

- CN and CPR are Crown-chartered corporations that now operate as **for-profit, US-market-focused** entities
- ~40-50% of revenue from US operations
- **Crude-by-rail and backhaul refined products** are profitable for the railway because the loop is inefficient for Canada
- No fast, affordable intercity passenger rail

### The Solution

| Project | Cost | Timeline | Model |
|---------|------|----------|-------|
| Toronto-Montreal high-speed rail | **$12-15B** | 10-15 years | Via Rail expansion + Crown construction |
| Calgary-Edmonton corridor | ~$5-8B | 8-10 years | Provincial partnership |
| Electrified freight corridors | ~$3-5B | 5-8 years | Reduce diesel dependency, CN/CPR corridor sharing |

### Impact

- Reduces **CN/CPR passenger and freight dependency**
- Creates construction and operating jobs that **do not offshore**
- Establishes a public transport alternative to **corporate airline and trucking oligopolies**

### The Problem

- Canadians pay some of the **highest drug prices in the OECD**
- **IMC lobbying** (150+ meetings, $161K StatCan payment) blocked PMPRB drug pricing reforms
- Dental care is **not universal** — 1 in 4 Canadians avoid dental care due to cost

### The Solution

| Program | Annual Cost | Revenue Source |
|---------|------------|---------------|
| National dental care | **$1.7B/year** | 4% of new corporate tax revenue |
| Pharmacare expansion | **$3-5B/year** | 7-11% of new revenue |
| PMPRB reform restoration | Regulatory (zero cost) | Reverse Duclos suspension |

### Impact

- **Bulk purchasing power** reduces per-unit drug costs
- Removing IMC's political capture restores **evidence-based pricing**
- Health outcomes improve → productivity gains → economic return

## The Counter-Arguments & Rebuttals

| Objection | Rebuttal |
|-----------|----------|
| "Corporations will leave." | See `CORPORATE_FLIGHT_REBUTTAL.md` — 90% flight required to break even; no historical precedent. If they leave, we buy their assets at discount. |
| "We can't afford it." | The $36.7B-$88B annual foregone value add and extraction (see `AGGREGATE_EXTRACTION.md`) demonstrates that Canada already *produces* the wealth — current policy simply directs it to corporate capture rather than public reinvestment. |
| "Government can't run businesses." | Hydro-Québec, SaskTel, ATB, and Caisses populaires demonstrate otherwise. The failures are political sabotage, not public ownership. |
| "It will take too long." | The proportionality clause trapped us for 30 years. USMCA removed it in 2020. The door is open — we just need to walk through it. |
| "Alberta will revolt like 1980." | The NEP failed because it was imposed, not because sovereignty is wrong. A *Crown refinery in Alberta* creates Alberta jobs and Alberta tax revenue — the opposite of the NEP's Ottawa-centric model. |
