# Canadian Financial Transparency Campaign

## A Citizen's Guide to Requesting Accountability from the Bank of Canada

---

## Purpose

This document provides templates and guidance for Canadian citizens who wish to exercise their right to access information about the Bank of Canada's operations. The goal is **transparency and accountability** — not conspiracy theories.

Every Canadian has the right to understand how public institutions operate, especially when those institutions manage hundreds of billions of dollars of public funds.

---

## Legitimate Areas of Inquiry

### 1. Market Concentration
The Big Five banks (RBC, TD, Scotiabank, BMO, CIBC) control the majority of Canada's financial system. This is a legitimate antitrust concern, not a conspiracy.

### 2. Housing Affordability
Banks hold the majority of Canadian mortgages. Understanding their influence on housing policy is a valid public interest question.

### 3. Monetary Policy Transparency
When the Bank of Canada makes decisions affecting every Canadian's mortgage, savings, and cost of living, citizens have a right to understand the decision-making process.

### 4. Regulatory Oversight
Understanding who regulates the banks, and whether those regulators have conflicts of interest, is a matter of democratic accountability.

## ATIP Request Templates

### Template 1: Board Member Backgrounds

**Subject:** Access to Information Request – Bank of Canada Board Composition

To: ATIP Officer, Bank of Canada

Dear Sir/Madam,

Pursuant to Section 4(1) of the Access to Information Act, I request the following:

1. A list of all current members of the Bank of Canada's Board of Directors, including their professional backgrounds and current affiliations.

2. Disclosure of any board members who have, in the past 10 years, been employed by or served on the board of any of the following institutions: RBC, TD Bank, Scotiabank, BMO, CIBC, or National Bank.

3. Any conflict-of-interest declarations filed by board members regarding their financial sector affiliations.

I am a Canadian citizen exercising my right to understand how public institutions are governed. This request is made in the public interest.

[Your name, address, signature]

## How to File

1. **Prepare your letter** using the templates above
2. **Print and sign** the document
3. **Pay the $5 fee** (cheque payable to Receiver General for Canada)
4. **Send via registered mail** to:
   
   Access to Information and Privacy Division
   Bank of Canada
   234 Wellington Street
   Ottawa, ON K1A 0G9

5. **Email a copy** to: ATIP@bankofcanada.ca

6. **Wait up to 30 days** for response

---

## If You Receive Redactions

If the Bank redacts information, you have the right to:

1. **File a complaint** with the Office of the Information Commissioner (OIC) within 30 days
2. **Request a judicial review** in Federal Court if the OIC rules in your favor but the Bank still refuses

The OIC is an independent body that investigates ATIP complaints. They have the authority to order disclosure.
