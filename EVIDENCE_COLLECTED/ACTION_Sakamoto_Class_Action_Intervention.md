## HOW TO INTERVENE

### Option 1: Amicus Curiae (Friend of the Court)

**What it is:** Non-party provides evidence to assist the court, without becoming a party.

**Advantages:** No liability exposure, no costs obligation, focus on evidence.

**Requirements:** Permission of the court; demonstrate expertise; show assistance to court.

**How to apply:**
1. File Motion for Leave to Intervene as Amicus Curiae
2. Include: interest, evidence to contribute, how it assists, confirmation of no delay

### Option 2: Intervener Status

**What it is:** Non-party with direct interest; full participation, discovery access, legal arguments.

**Advantages:** Full participation, access to discovery documents.

**Disadvantages:** Potential costs liability, complex procedural requirements.

### Option 3: Evidence Submission (No Formal Intervention)

**What it is:** Provide evidence to plaintiffs' counsel for use in their case.

**Advantages:** Simplest, no court involvement, no liability.

**How:** Contact plaintiffs' lawyers directly, offer compiled evidence, sign confidentiality agreements if needed.
