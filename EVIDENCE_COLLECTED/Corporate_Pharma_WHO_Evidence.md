## 🎯 KEY FINDINGS SUMMARY

### **Finding 1: Pfizer COVID Vaccine Contracts - Secret Spending**

**Evidence Level:** HIGH - Auditor General reports, parliamentary records

#### Contract Details:
- **~$5 billion** spent on ~169 million vaccine doses (Dec 2020 - May 2022)
- **~$9 billion** total budgeted for vaccine procurement (2021 budget)
- **Confidentiality clauses** prevented contract disclosure - Minister Anita Anand repeatedly cited secrecy
- Canada paid **premium prices** to get doses earlier than planned
- EU accidentally revealed prices: Pfizer $18.40/dose, Moderna $22.91/dose (Belgian politician tweet)
- Average cost estimated at **$30 per dose** based on unclassified documentation

#### Vaccine Wastage (Auditor General Reports):
- **32.5 million doses** in inventory worth ~$1 billion (May 2022)
- **14.4 million doses destroyed** in 2023 due to expiration
- **12 million doses wasted** in 2022
- **50.6 million doses deemed surplus** - only 15.3 million actually donated
- **13.6 million doses expired** waiting for donation
- PHAC knew oversupply was likely outcome but kept purchasing optional doses
- VaccineConnect tracking system purchased from Deloitte for $60 million had delays

#### Total Estimated Waste: $1 billion+ in expired vaccines

### **Finding 2: Health Minister Duclos - Pharma Lobbying Intervention**

**Evidence Level:** HIGH - ATIP documents, parliamentary committee evidence

#### The Intervention:
- Health Minister **Jean-Yves Duclos** personally intervened in November 2022
- Asked drug price regulator (PMPRB) to **suspend reforms** after 6 years of delay
- Reforms would have saved Canadians **billions in drug costs**
- Duclos' letter was described as **"indistinguishable from pharmaceutical lobby talking points"**

#### Lobbying Timeline:
- **Nov 1, 2022:** IMC president Pamela Fralick sent letter to regulator
- **Nov 18, 2022:** Fralick met with Health policy advisor Chris Zhou
- **Nov 18, 2022:** Fralick sent another letter asking for meeting with regulator
- **Nov 28, 2022:** Fralick received Duclos' letter AND IMC's letter on same day
- **Dec 1, 2022:** Fralick in touch with Duclos' chief of staff and senior advisors
- **Dec 5, 2022:** Health ministry official submitted request for delay using language similar to minister's

#### The Result:
- New drug price guidelines suspended indefinitely
- **No reforms implemented** to "protect Canadians from excessive drug prices"
- IMC then requested ongoing **"quarterly meetings"** with regulator
- Government officials then tried to **suppress documents** showing the intervention

#### Source Documents:
- ATIP documents obtained by The Breach
- Parliamentary committee testimony (HESA)
- Lobby registry records

### **Finding 3: Innovative Medicines Canada - Paid Government Reports**

**Evidence Level:** HIGH - ATIP documents, StatCan records

#### The Scheme:
- Canada's top pharmaceutical lobby group **paid Statistics Canada** to produce reports
- **$161,072** paid for two reports published in 2020-2021
- Reports painted **flattering picture** of pharma's economic contribution
- **IMC had final approval** over indicators and input into writing
- Statistics Canada **did not disclose** that lobby group paid for and shaped reports
- IMC used reports in **lobbying and PR campaigns** without disclosing funding

#### The Deception:
- Reports published on government website appeared official
- Used by IMC in **pre-budget consultations** with Alberta, Ontario, and federal governments
- Used to criticize Canada's drug price regulator in news releases
- "Decision-based evidence-making" according to health economist Steve Morgan

### **Finding 4: Revolving Door - Pharma to Government to Pharma**

**Evidence Level:** HIGH - Public records, lobby registry, appointment records

#### Thomas Digby - PMPRB Chair Appointment:
- **Feb 1, 2023:** Appointed Chair of Patented Medicine Prices Review Board
- **Pharma IP lawyer** with 25+ years in pharmaceutical/biotech sectors
- Task: Regulate drug prices to prevent excessive pricing
- **Conflict:** His career was built protecting pharmaceutical intellectual property
- Innovative Medicines Canada publicly welcomed his appointment
- Board member resigned citing concerns about panel's independence

#### Pamela Fralick - IMC President:
- **Former Deputy CEO/Director of Liaison and Coordination at Health Canada** (1991-1997)
- Now president of pharmaceutical lobby group
- Regularly lobbies her former department
- Had multiple meetings with current Health Minister's staff

#### Bettina Hamelin - IMC President (Current):
- **Former Vice-President at Natural Sciences and Engineering Research Council** (NSERC)
- Held public office 2015-2017
- Now lobbies government on behalf of pharmaceutical companies

#### The Pattern:
- Government officials move to pharma → Lobby former colleagues
- Pharma lawyers appointed to regulate pharma → No independent oversight
- "Revolving door" creates regulatory capture

### **Finding 8: Drug Price Reform Failure - Corporate Win**

**Evidence Level:** HIGH - Multiple investigative reports, parliamentary records

#### The Story:
- Liberals promised in **2017** to lower Canada's drug prices (among highest in world)
- Pharma industry launched **court challenges** and threatened to withhold drugs
- Reforms delayed for **6 years** through industry resistance
- In final days of 2022, Minister Duclos intervened to suspend them
- **No drug price reforms implemented** as of 2026
- Canadians continue paying some of highest drug prices globally

#### Regulatory Capture Evidence:
- PMPRB acting chair warned pharma would never agree to price constraints
- Memo: "After 5 years, myriad policy proposals and many hundreds of hours of consultation, it would appear the pharmaceutical industry is simply not amenable to any measures that would further constrain its ability to sell patented medicines in Canada at free market prices"
- Despite this warning, minister still asked for more delay
- NDP accused Duclos of "protecting profits of very profitable pharmaceutical industries"
