**Address:** 130 Colonnade Road, Ottawa, ON K1A 0K9
**Email:** phac.atip-aiprp.aspc@canada.ca

---

### Request

Pursuant to the *Access to Information Act*, R.S.C., 1985, c. A-1, I request access to the following records:

**1. VISP Claim Statistics (June 1, 2021 – present)**

All records, including but not limited to:
- (a) The total number of Vaccine Injury Support Program (VISP) claims received
- (b) The number of claims approved, denied, and pending decision
- (c) The total amount of compensation paid to claimants
- (d) The breakdown of claims by vaccine type (COVID-19 vs. other vaccines)
- (e) The breakdown of adverse event types listed in approved claims
- (f) The average processing time from application to decision
- (g) The number of claims that exceeded the predicted annual volume of 400 claims per year

**2. VISP Denial Records**

All records, including but not limited to:
- (a) The number of claims denied on the basis that the injury was "unrelated to the vaccine"
- (b) The criteria used to determine causation between vaccine and injury
- (c) The identity (by position, not personal name) of the medical reviewers making causation determinations
- (d) Any internal correspondence regarding the denial of claims for injuries listed in vaccine product monographs as known adverse events

**3. VISP Budget and Forecasting Records**

All records, including but not limited to:
- (a) The initial budget allocated to VISP at program launch (2021)
- (b) The internal forecasts for annual claim volumes prepared by PHAC and/or Oxaro
- (c) Any records showing when PHAC/Oxaro first recognized that actual claim volumes exceeded forecasts
- (d) Any records of requests for additional funding or staffing due to claim volume increases

---

### Legal Basis for Disclosure

I note the following:

1. **Litigation privilege has expired.** The Supreme Court of Canada held in *Blank v. Canada (Minister of Justice)*, [2006] 2 SCR 319, that litigation privilege expires when the litigation that gave rise to it terminates. The COVID-19 pandemic emergency has ended. There is no ongoing "emergency" litigation that would support a claim of litigation privilege over VISP records.

2. **Public interest in disclosure.** The public has a right to know the scale of vaccine injuries acknowledged by the government's own compensation program. As of June 2025, media reports indicate over 3,317 claims have been filed — far exceeding initial predictions of 40–400 per year. This information is necessary for informed public debate about vaccine safety and government transparency.

3. **No commercial third-party exemption applies.** These are government administrative records, not third-party commercial information under s. 20(1) of the Act.

## TEMPLATE 2: Unredacted Pfizer/BioNTech Contract (Cite: Merck Frosst + PHMPT v. FDA)

### To
**Institution:** Public Services and Procurement Canada (PSPC)
**ATIP Coordinator:** ATIP Coordinator, Public Services and Procurement Canada
**Address:** 11 Laurier Street, Gatineau, QC K1A 0S5
**Email:** ATIP-AIPRP@tpsgc-pwgsc.gc.ca

---

### Request

Pursuant to the *Access to Information Act*, R.S.C., 1985, c. A-1, I request access to the following records:

**1. Pfizer-BioNTech Vaccine Supply Contract(s)**

All records relating to the contract or contracts between the Government of Canada and Pfizer Inc., Pfizer Canada Inc., BioNTech SE, and/or any of their affiliates for the supply of COVID-19 vaccines, including but not limited to:

- (a) The complete, unredacted text of all supply agreements, purchase orders, and amendments
- (b) Pricing information, including per-dose cost, total contract value, and any price adjustment mechanisms
- (c) Delivery terms, incoterms, and schedule
- (d) Liability and indemnification clauses, including any provisions limiting Pfizer's liability for vaccine injuries
- (e) Intellectual property provisions, including any provisions relating to patent rights, licensing, or technology transfer
- (f) Confidentiality provisions and their scope
- (g) Any provisions relating to adverse event reporting obligations by Pfizer to the Government of Canada

**2. Related Correspondence**

All records of correspondence between the Government of Canada and Pfizer/BioNTech relating to the above contracts, including:
- (a) Negotiation records
- (b) Amendments or side letters
- (c) Dispute resolution records

---

### Legal Basis for Disclosure

I note the following:

1. **Public interest outweighs commercial confidentiality.** The Supreme Court of Canada in *Merck Frosst Canada Ltd. v. Canada (Health)*, [2012] 1 SCR 23, held that the *Access to Information Act* "strikes a careful balance between the public interest in transparency and the need to protect confidential commercial information."

   In this case, the public interest in knowing the terms of a contract for vaccines that were:
   - Purchased with public funds
   - Administered to over 80% of Canadians
   - The subject of ongoing safety concerns and litigation
   
   ...far outweighs any residual commercial confidentiality interest.

2. **The contract is already public in other jurisdictions.** The European Commission's contract with Pfizer-BioNTech was published by Italian broadcaster RAI in April 2021. The contract is already in the public domain in the European Union. There is no legitimate commercial reason to keep the Canadian version secret when the identical or substantially similar document has been published elsewhere.

   Citation: Public Citizen, "A Piece of the COVID Vaccine Recipe" (2021): "In November 2020, the European Commission signed an agreement with Pfizer and BioNTech to purchase 100 million doses... In April 2021, the Italian public broadcaster RAI published the EC Contract."

3. **U.S. precedent mandates disclosure.** In *Public Health and Medical Professionals for Transparency v. FDA*, N.D. Texas Case No. 353278, the U.S. District Court ordered the FDA to produce all data relating to the Pfizer vaccine, ruling in December 2024:

   > "The Covid-19 pandemic is long passed and so has any legitimate reason for concealing from the American people the information relied upon by the government in approving the Pfizer Vaccine."

   If the U.S. court has ordered disclosure of vaccine approval data, the Canadian public has at least an equivalent right to know the terms under which the Government of Canada purchased those same vaccines.

4. **Public procurement transparency.** The contract was funded with public money. Canadian taxpayers have a right to know what they paid for, on what terms, and with what liability protections for the manufacturer.

**3. Post-Decision Communications**

All records of communications between the Office of the Minister of Health (Jean-Yves Duclos) and any of the entities listed in section 2 above, for the period July 1, 2022 – December 31, 2022.

**4. Briefing Notes**

All briefing notes, memos, or decision documents prepared for the Minister of Health regarding the PMPRB drug pricing reforms, for the period January 1, 2022 – December 31, 2022.

---

### Legal Basis for Disclosure

1. **Ministerial records transferred to department are within ATIP scope.** The Supreme Court of Canada in *Canada (Information Commissioner) v. Canada (Minister of National Defence)*, [2011] 2 SCR 306, held that records in ministerial offices are not under institutional control. However, once records are transferred from a minister's office to a government department (e.g., from the Minister of Health's office to Health Canada), they become subject to ATIP. This request targets departmental records, not ministerial office records.

2. **No solicitor-client privilege.** These are policy and administrative records, not legal advice.

3. **Public interest in accountability.** The public has a right to know whether the Minister of Health was lobbied by pharmaceutical companies before intervening to suspend drug price reforms that would have saved Canadian patients hundreds of millions of dollars.

## FILING INSTRUCTIONS

### How to File an ATIP Request in Canada

**Step 1: Choose the Institution**
- VISP data → Public Health Agency of Canada (PHAC)
- Pfizer contracts → Public Services and Procurement Canada (PSPC)
- Duclos communications → Health Canada

**Step 2: Submit Online (Recommended)**
- Visit: https://www.canada.ca/en/treasury-board-secretariat/services/access-information-privacy/access-information/access-information-request-form.html
- Use the online form for faster processing

**Step 3: Submit by Email**
- Find the ATIP coordinator email for each institution
- Include the full text of your request
- Include your contact information

**Step 4: Submit by Mail**
- Send to the ATIP Coordinator at the institution's address
- Keep a copy and proof of mailing

**Step 5: Track Your Request**
- The institution must acknowledge receipt within 5 business days
- The standard response time is 30 days (may be extended with notice)
- If denied, you can complain to the Office of the Information Commissioner

### Tips for Success

- **Be specific** about the records you want
- **Cite the legal basis** for disclosure (as done in these templates)
- **Mention the public interest** explicitly
- **Request electronic format** to avoid paper copying fees
- **Set a fee limit** ($25–$50 is standard)
- **Keep copies** of everything you send
- **Follow up** if you don't hear back within 30 days
