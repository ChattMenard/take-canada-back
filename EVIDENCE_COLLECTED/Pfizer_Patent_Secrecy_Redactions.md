## FINDING 1: FDA Redacted Pfizer Vaccine Ingredients — FOIA Required to Reveal

### Evidence

**ICAN FOIA Request (September 14, 2021):**
- The Informed Consent Action Network (ICAN), represented by attorney Aaron Siri, filed a FOIA request with the FDA
- Request sought: "a copy of page 8 of the document available at https://www.fda.gov/media/151733/download, **without any redaction of the ingredients listed at the top of that page**"
- The FDA's approval document for the Pfizer-BioNTech vaccine (COMIRNATY) had **redacted the ingredients list**
- Source: ICAN FOIA Request IR#0558, September 2021

**What This Means:**
- The FDA approved a vaccine for injection into billions of people but **redacted the ingredients list** from the public approval document
- A non-profit organization had to file a federal FOIA request to try to get the list of what was actually in the vaccine
- The ingredient redaction was on page 8 of the FDA approval document

## FINDING 2: UK Government Redacted Pfizer Contract Under "Commercial Interests"

### Evidence

**UK Information Commissioner Decision (2023):**
- Complainant requested: "Please provide a copy of the contract signed with Pfizer to supply Covid-19 Vaccinations."
- UK Health Security Agency (UKHSA) provided links to **redacted copies** of contracts
- Relied on FOIA Section 43(2) — "Commercial interests" exemption
- Source: UK ICO Decision Reference: FER0733482 / UKICO:2023:159581

**What Was Redacted:**
- Price information
- Incoterms (delivery terms)
- Indemnities (liability protections)
- Liabilities
- Other terms and conditions
- Personal information of signatories

**UKHSA's Justification:**
> "The release of information agreed between parties as confidential would cause unwarranted reputational damage to UKHSA as future suppliers would not be confident that their commercially confidential information will be kept confidential."

**The Information Commissioner's Finding:**
- Accepted that price, terms, indemnities were "commercially sensitive"
- BUT ordered disclosure of some personal data (names of signatories)
- Source: https://www.bailii.org/uk/cases/UKICO/2023/159581.pdf

## FINDING 3: FDA Fought in Court to Keep Pfizer Data Secret for 75 Years

### Evidence

**Public Health and Medical Professionals for Transparency v. FDA (N.D. Texas):**
- August 2021: Plaintiff submitted FOIA request for "All data and information for the Pfizer Vaccine" including:
  - Safety and effectiveness data
  - Protocols for tests/studies
  - Adverse reaction reports
  - **"A list of all active ingredients and any inactive ingredients"**
  - Assay methods
  - All correspondence with FDA
  - Manufacturer's testing of every lot
- The FDA proposed releasing documents at a rate of **500 pages per month** — which would have taken **75 years** to release all documents
- Source: Court documents, N.D. Texas Fort Worth Division

**FDA's Defense:**
> "confidential business and trade secret information of Pfizer or BioNTech and personal privacy information of patients who participated in clinical trials. FDA is required to protect certain information under the law and this type of information is exempt from production under the FOIA."
> — FDA Court Filing

**The Court's Ruling (December 2024):**
- Court found the FDA improperly withheld documents
- Ordered FDA to produce EUA (Emergency Use Authorization) documents
- Ruled EUA was incorporated into the BLA (Biologics License Application) and was responsive to FOIA request
- Source: https://fingfx.thomsonreuters.com/gfx/legaldocs/lgpdjabmbpo/Public%20Health%20and%20Medical%20Professionals%20for%20Transparency%20v%20FDA%20-%20order%20-%2020241206.pdf

## FINDING 4: Public Citizen Found "Recipe" Only Because European Contract Leaked

### Evidence

**Public Citizen Analysis (2021):**
> "The U.S. Food and Drug Administration (FDA) has only released heavily redacted specifications. The European Medicines Agency (EMA) has released information about quality attributes and analytical procedures, but not acceptance criteria."

- Public Citizen identified manufacturing specs from a **leaked European Commission contract** signed in November 2020
- The Italian public broadcaster **RAI published the EC contract** in April 2021
- This leak revealed what FDA had redacted from U.S. documents
- The leaked contract contained manufacturing specifications, quality attributes, and analytical procedures
- Source: https://www.citizen.org/article/a-piece-of-the-covid-vaccine-recipe/

**What This Shows:**
- The FDA was releasing "heavily redacted specifications" to Americans
- The full manufacturing information only became public because of a European media leak
- Pfizer/BioNTech manufacturing process remained secret even after approval

## FINDING 5: Patent Numbers Redacted in SEC Filings

### Evidence

**Nature Biotechnology Patent Network Analysis (2021):**
> "SEC filings highlighted by Knowledge Ecology International reveal a series of sublicenses for mRNA-related patents that stem from the University of Pennsylvania to both Moderna and BioNTech."
> "The 2017 filings indicate that the University of Pennsylvania exclusively licensed their patents to mRNA RiboTherapeutics, which then sublicensed them to its affiliate CellScript. CellScript proceeded to sublicense the patents to Moderna and BioNTech; **however, the patent numbers are redacted in all the filings**, making it difficult to determine which are relevant to the production of COVID-19 vaccines."
- Source: https://www.nature.com/articles/s41587-021-00912-9

**What This Means:**
- The patent sublicensing agreements that made COVID vaccines possible were filed with the SEC
- But **the actual patent numbers were redacted** in the public filings
- This made it impossible for the public to know which patents were being used and who owned them

## FINDING 6: Manufacturing Process is a Trade Secret

### Evidence

**Harvard Petrie-Flom Center (2022):**
> "COVID vaccine [Pfizer and Moderna] was one of the very first times that people have used a combination of the mRNA and nano particle — tiny container — technologies. **That's what makes it so difficult for others to replicate that manufacturing process, because it's still very secretive** from the outside."
> — Interview with intellectual property expert

**PMC Article on Compulsory Licensing:**
> "This requires the production of a 'well-defined mix of solid nanoparticles with consistent mRNA encapsulation.' This, in turn, requires a bespoke microfilter device that enables the manufacture of very precisely created nanoparticles."
- The exact manufacturing process for the lipid nanoparticle encapsulation remains a **trade secret**
- Without the "recipe," other manufacturers cannot replicate the vaccine even if they have the patents
- Source: https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC8690222/
