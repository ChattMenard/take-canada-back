## STRATEGY 1: ETHICS COMPLAINT (FILE FIRST)

**Target:** Jean-Yves Duclos
**Law:** Conflict of Interest Act, s. 9
**Precedent:** Trudeau II Report (2019)

**Why file first:**
- No lawyer required
- No costs
- Creates a public record
- The Trudeau II Report is a direct precedent

**How to file:**
1. Open `ACTION_Draft_Ethics_Complaint_Duclos.md`
2. Replace [bracketed] fields with your information
3. Attach supporting documents (ATIP releases, media reports)
4. Submit online: https://ciec-ccie.parl.gc.ca/
5. Email: info@ccec-ccie.parl.gc.ca
6. Mail: Office of the Conflict of Interest and Ethics Commissioner, Parliament of Canada, Office 1200, 131 Queen Street, Ottawa, ON K1A 0A6

**Key evidence:**
- Duclos' June 17, 2022 letter to PMPRB
- IMC 150+ lobbying meetings
- $161,000 StatCan payment
- Revolving door (Fralick, Hamelin, Digby)

## STRATEGY 2: ATIP REQUESTS (FILE SECOND)

**Three ready-to-file templates:**

### Template 1: VISP Claim Data
- **Target:** Public Health Agency of Canada (PHAC)
- **Request:** All VISP claim statistics, denial records, budget/forecasting
- **Legal cite:** Blank v. Canada — litigation privilege expired
- **Email:** phac.atip-aiprp.aspc@canada.ca

### Template 2: Pfizer/BioNTech Contract
- **Target:** Public Services and Procurement Canada (PSPC)
- **Request:** Complete unredacted supply contract(s)
- **Legal cite:** Merck Frosst + PHMPT v. FDA (US court ordered disclosure)
- **Email:** ATIP-AIPRP@tpsgc-pwgsc.gc.ca

### Template 3: Duclos-PMPRB Communications
- **Target:** Health Canada
- **Request:** Duclos letter, IMC communications, briefing notes
- **Legal cite:** Canada v. Minister of Defence — departmental records are ATIP-able
- **Email:** ATIP-AIPRP@hc-sc.gc.ca

**How to file:**
- Online: https://www.canada.ca/en/treasury-board-secretariat/services/access-information-privacy/access-information/access-information-request-form.html
- Or email the ATIP coordinator directly
- Fee limit: $25–$50

## STRATEGY 3: COMPETITION ACT COMPLAINT

**Target:** Innovative Medicines Canada (IMC) + member companies
**Law:** Competition Act, ss. 45, 46, 79
**Precedent:** R. v. Carson (SCC — influence peddling conviction)

**Allegation:**
IMC coordinated a lobbying campaign to suppress drug price competition, maintaining high prices for Canadian patients.

**How to file:**
1. Draft complaint using framework in `ACTION_Competition_Act_Complaint_Framework.md`
2. Include evidence: lobbying registry, Duclos letter, OECD price data
3. Submit to Competition Bureau:
   - Email: information@cb-bc.gc.ca
   - Phone: 1-800-348-5358
   - Mail: 50 Victoria Street, Gatineau, QC K1A 0C9
4. Also file with Lobbying Commissioner: info@lobbycanada.gc.ca

## STRATEGY 5: PRIVATE PROSECUTION (HOLD)

**Target:** Revolving door officials (e.g., Michel Fralick)
**Law:** Criminal Code, s. 121(1)(d) — influence peddling
**Precedent:** R. v. Carson (SCC)

**Recommendation:** DO NOT pursue at this stage.

**Why:**
- Extremely high evidentiary burden
- Crown will almost certainly stay charges
- Resources better spent on Strategies 1–4

**When to reconsider:**
- If a "smoking gun" emerges (e.g., email showing quid pro quo)
- After ATIP requests yield documentary evidence
- After Ethics Complaint or Competition Act investigation produces findings

**What to do instead:**
- Build the case through civil/administrative channels
- Advocate for legislative reform (cooling-off periods, transparency)
- Use the framework as a deterrent and strategic threat
