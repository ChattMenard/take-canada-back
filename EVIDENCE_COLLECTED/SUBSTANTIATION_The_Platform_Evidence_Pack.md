## CLAIM 9: Moderna's "66-Day" Vaccine Timeline

### Evidence

**New England Journal of Medicine (Preliminary Report):**
> "Vaccine development was initiated after the SARS-CoV-2 genome was posted on January 10, 2020; manufacture and delivery of clinical trials material was completed within 45 days, and the first trial participants were vaccinated on March 16, 2020, just 66 days after the genomic sequence of the virus was released."
> Source: https://www.nejm.org/doi/full/10.1056/NEJMoa2022483

**bioRxiv Preprint (NIAID/Moderna):**
> "Within 5 days of sequence release, current Good Manufacturing Practice (cGMP) production of mRNA/LNP expressing the SARS-CoV-2 S-2P... was initiated in parallel with preclinical evaluation."
> "This led to the start of a first in human Phase 1 clinical trial on March 16, 2020, 66 days after the viral sequence was released."
> Source: https://www.biorxiv.org/content/10.1101/2020.06.11.145920v1.full.pdf

**Context:**
- Standard vaccine development: 5-10 years
- Moderna's COVID vaccine: 66 days from sequence to human trials
- This speed was only possible because the platform (mRNA + LNP) was already built and tested
