## The Oath Continuity Question

### The User's Situation

> *"What about people like me, who used to be sworn to their king, who have not been re-sworn to their new king, whom I readily recognize as my new leader?"*

This is the central question. If you swore allegiance to Queen Elizabeth II, does that oath survive her death? Or are you left oathless until formally re-sworn?

### The Legal Answer: You Are Not Oathless

The key phrase in Canadian oaths is **"Heirs and Successors."**

The Canadian citizenship oath now reads:

> *"I swear (or affirm) that I will be faithful and bear true allegiance to His Majesty King Charles III, King of Canada, His Heirs and Successors."*

The Canadian Armed Forces enrolment oath historically stated allegiance to:

> *"Queen Elizabeth II, Queen of Canada, her heirs and successors according to law, and service until lawfully released."*

**Critical distinction:** The oath was not to Elizabeth as a private person. It was to **the Crown in continuity** — the lawful monarch who succeeds her.

When Queen Elizabeth II died, King Charles III automatically became Sovereign of Canada. The Governor General's office describes him as King of Canada and Canada's Head of State — the personal embodiment of the Canadian Crown.

**Your allegiance flows to the new Sovereign automatically.** You were not left oathless because nobody gathered everyone in a room to repeat it.

### DND Confirmation

After Elizabeth II's death, the Department of National Defence confirmed:

- Some changes happened immediately upon King Charles III's accession
- The oath of allegiance is now sworn to the King
- This was automatic, not requiring mass re-ceremonies

## Semper Fi: What It Actually Means

### The Etymology

**"Fi" is not a separate Latin word.** It is the compressed, carried weight of **Fidelis**.

| Latin | Meaning |
|-------|---------|
| **fides** | faith, trust, loyalty, reliability |
| **fidelis** | faithful, loyal, trustworthy, true |
| **fidelity** | faithfulness, loyalty, keeping trust |
| **fiduciary** | someone entrusted with responsibility |
| **confide** | to trust someone with something |
| **confidence** | trust, firm belief |
| **infidel** | literally "not faithful" / unbeliever |

### Semper Fidelis → Semper Fi

**Semper Fidelis** = "Always faithful."
**Semper Fi** = "Always faithful," compressed into a call-sign, greeting, promise, identity, and recognition.

The full phrase explains. The short version **signals**.

### What It Actually Means

Not "always obedient."
Not "always owned."
Not "always useful to the machine."

**Always faithful.**

- To the oath.
- To the trust.
- To the people.
- To the good.
- To what was sacred before the institution got involved.
