## HOW TO READ THIS MATRIX

Each row is a **claim**. Each column is a **category of evidence**.

- **Primary Source (Govt/Court):** Government documents, court filings, official reports, legislation
- **Primary Source (Corporate):** SEC filings, company disclosures, contracts
- **Peer-Reviewed:** Academic journals, scientific publications
- **Investigative Media:** Established outlets with document-based reporting
- **FOIA/ATIP Release:** Documents obtained through freedom of information requests
- **Leak:** Unauthorized disclosure of otherwise secret documents
- **Cross-Jurisdiction:** The same pattern observed in multiple countries

**Verification Levels:**
- **✓✓✓ (3+ sources, cross-category):** Highly reliable. Multiple independent types of evidence converge.
- **✓✓ (2 sources, same or different category):** Solid. Corroborated but not fully cross-category.
- **✓ (1 source):** Documented but not independently corroborated. Needs confirmation.

## HOW TO USE THIS MATRIX

### When Someone Asks "Prove It"

Point them to the ✓✓✓ claims. These have **multiple independent types of evidence** converging on the same conclusion. Example:

> "You claim the FDA hid the ingredients. Prove it."

> "Three independent sources: (1) The FDA's own approval document has redactions on page 8. (2) ICAN filed a FOIA request specifically for that page. (3) Multiple investigative outlets reported on it. That's ✓✓✓."

### When Someone Says "That's Just a Conspiracy Theory"

Show them the **cross-category verification**. A claim supported by government documents + court filings + peer-reviewed research + investigative media is not a theory. It is a documented pattern.

### When You Need to Prioritize Research

Focus on the ✓ claims. These need additional verification. Strengthening them raises the overall reliability of the evidence base.
