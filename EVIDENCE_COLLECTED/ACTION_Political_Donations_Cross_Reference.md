# ACTION: Political Donations & Policy Votes Cross-Reference Framework

## Mapping Corporate Money to Legislative Outcomes

This document provides a methodology and template for cross-referencing corporate political donations, lobbying expenditures, and revolving-door appointments with the policy votes and regulatory decisions that benefit those same corporations.

---

## The Thesis

**Corporate profit → Political donations + lobbying → Favorable policy → More profit → Loop repeats**

This is not conspiracy theory. It is documented, trackable, and provable through public records. The goal of this framework is to build a **replicable, evidence-based methodology** for connecting money to outcomes.

## Methodology: From Data to Evidence

### Step 1: Harvest Donation Data

1. Download CSVs from Elections Canada for relevant years (2015, 2019, 2021, 2025 federal elections)
2. Filter for donations from target entities (CN, CPR, Irving, Suncor, Imperial, banks, IMC members)
3. Note: Corporate donations are banned at federal level since 2006, but **corporate executives, PACs, and riding associations** still channel money
4. Provincial and municipal levels often still allow corporate donations

### Step 2: Map Timeline

Create a spreadsheet with the following columns:

| Date | Entity | Donation Amount | Recipient (Party/Candidate) | Policy Decision Date | Policy Decision | Beneficiary | Lag (Days) |
|------|--------|----------------|---------------------------|---------------------|-----------------|-------------|------------|
| 2021-09-15 | IMC (via exec) | $5,000 | Liberal Riding Association | 2022-06-17 | Duclos suspends PMPRB | IMC members | 275 |
| 2019-03-01 | CN (via PAC) | $10,000 | Conservative Party | 2019-04-15 | Pipeline regulatory decision | CN (rail competition) | 45 |

### Step 3: Establish Correlation

**What counts as evidence:**

- **Direct correlation:** Donation within 12 months of a policy decision that directly benefits the donor
- **Pattern:** Recurring donations to the same party before multiple favorable decisions
- **Concentration:** Unusual spike in donations from an industry before a specific vote
- **Revolving door:** Official leaves government → joins donor corporation → policy shifts in their former portfolio

**What does NOT count as evidence:**

- Donations to parties that never win (unless strategic)
- Donations with no corresponding policy action
- Single donations without pattern

### Step 4: Calculate Ratios

| Metric | Formula | Example |
|--------|---------|---------|
| **Return on political investment** | (Policy benefit $) / (Donation $) | $161M StatCan contract / $50K donations = 3,220:1 |
| **Donation density** | (Industry donations in year) / (Total donations that year) | Pharma donations / all corporate donations |
| **Policy lag** | (Decision date) – (Donation date) | Average days between donation and favorable decision |

## ATIP Targets for Donation Data

Some donation data is not fully public. File ATIPs for:

1. **Elections Canada:** Complete raw donation database (aggregated by donor name, not personal info)
2. **Lobbying Commissioner:** Complete lobbying meeting records with ministers (not just senior officials)
3. **Finance Canada:** Internal correspondence regarding corporate tax policy and donation feedback
4. **Provincial election authorities:** Raw donation data for Alberta, Ontario, Quebec, New Brunswick
5. **Municipal clerks:** Developer donation records linked to zoning votes (Edmonton, Toronto, Vancouver)

## Template: Evidence Submission Package

When you find a correlation, document it in this format:

### Claim

[Entity] donated $[X] to [Recipient] on [Date]. Within [Y] days/weeks/months, [Policy Decision] was made, benefiting [Entity] by approximately $[Z].

### Evidence

- **Donation record:** [Elections Canada / provincial registry link or screenshot]
- **Policy record:** [Hansard, ministerial letter, regulatory filing]
- **Benefit quantification:** [Contract value, tax savings, revenue increase]
- **Timeline:** [Spreadsheet row or visual timeline]

### Confidence Level

- ★★★★★ **Smoking gun:** Email or memo explicitly linking donation to policy
- ★★★★☆ **Strong correlation:** Donation + policy + no other plausible explanation
- ★★★☆☆ **Suggestive:** Donation + policy + competing explanations exist
- ★★☆☆☆ **Weak:** Single data point, no pattern
- ★☆☆☆☆ **Coincidence:** No meaningful correlation
