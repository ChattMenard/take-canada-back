### Government Contracts and Conflicts

#### Historical Conflict (2001-2005)
- **2001:** Canada Post purchased 50% of Intelcom Courier Inc. for $1 million
- **Owner at time:** Daniel Hudon - Liberal fundraiser, former member of finance committee for Quebec wing of federal Liberal Party
- **Canada Post President:** André Ouellet - former Liberal cabinet minister, Jean Chrétien's political minister for Quebec
- **Justice Minister:** Martin Cauchon - Hudon's friend, president of Liberal Quebec wing (1993-1995)
- **Allegations:** Canada Post pressured suppliers to switch to Intelcom; Canada Post officials accompanied Intelcom sales reps to meetings
- **Opposition Response:** Canadian Alliance MP Gerry Ritz called for investigation; Transport Minister David Collenette refused
- **Evaluation:** Deloitte & Touche (also Canada Post's auditor) approved the deal

#### Modern Connection (2015-present)
- **2015:** Jean-Sébastien Joly (brother of Mélanie Joly) became CEO of Intelcom
- **2017:** Jean-Sébastien Joly bought Intelcom with support from CDPQ (Quebec pension fund)
- **Current:** CDPQ remains one of Intelcom's main shareholders
- **Amazon Partnership:** Intelcom is major Amazon subcontractor for last-mile delivery
- **2024 Postal Strike:** Canada Post used Intelcom as strikebreaker/subcontractor during postal workers' strike
- **2025:** Amazon closed Quebec warehouses; Intelcom took over operations, causing delivery delays
- **Consumer Complaints:** Quebec consumer protection bureau received 7 complaints against Intelcom by 2022
